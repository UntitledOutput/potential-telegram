R

Representation

Students deserve a real seat at the table.

Amplify Student Voice.

  • Create the largest and most diverse SMOB advisory council MCPS has ever seen, including not just middle and high schoolers, but even elementary schoolers interested in advocacy and the SMOB process.
  • Create an anonymous student submission form for anyone to share their concerns, from personal to countywide issues.
  • Propose legislation on behalf of youth groups in MCPS, such as MCR, MCJC, Student FAIR, Eco MoCo, MoCo4Change, and school clubs to ensure a diverse set of concerns are voiced.

Maintain Communication.

  • Bring back the SMOB monthly minute, though with a stronger emphasis on utilizing social media to ensure people see visible change, and not just email updates.
  • Visit ALL middle and high schools at least once throughout my tenure as SMOB.

Ensure Transparency in Decision-Making.

  • Release student impact statements for every major MCPS decision, including boundary changes, grading policies, and security measures.
  • Clearly explain why each decision was passed, who may benefit or be harmed, and how student feedback influenced the outcome.
