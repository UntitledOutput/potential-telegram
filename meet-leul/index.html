Meet Leul

“Loo-El?” “Le-Ool?”

My name has been butchered and mispronounced so many times that I know I’m next whenever a teacher says, “I’m sorry if I mess this next name up,” during attendance. Even though it kills me every time, it’s also what makes me unique—and I’ve learned to appreciate that.

Since my fourth-grade teacher first introduced the idea of running for my elementary school’s SGA Vice President in 2019, advocacy has been a constant in my life. Whether it was lobbying in Annapolis before the Maryland General Assembly or simply amplifying the work of my peers, I’ve spent the better half of the past six years pushing for reform, change, and evolution in my community.

For a long time, seeing former SMOBs come from areas I had never even heard of discouraged me. It made me feel like I didn’t belong, like my voice mattered less just because I came from somewhere different.

Ironically, that difference is what brought me here. It’s what shaped my perspective and pushed me to speak up. I never want another student to feel the way I did when I first entered advocacy. Every student deserves to feel seen, valued, and equal, both inside and outside of school.

There should never be a reason for hatred or animosity to grow so strong that it leads to violence within our communities. My goal is to help ensure our schools are not only safe, but supportive, adequately resourced to serve students and programs that promote equity, belonging, and opportunity.

As SMOB, I will work to make sure every student knows their voice matters, no matter their background, their school, or how difficult their name might be to pronounce. Our differences should never divide us. They should be what make us stronger.

#LEUL4SMOB.
